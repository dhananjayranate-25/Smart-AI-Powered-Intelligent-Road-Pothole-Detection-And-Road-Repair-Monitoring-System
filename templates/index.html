What is a Pothole?

A pothole is a depression or cavity in a road surface, typically caused by water seeping into cracks in the pavement, freezing and expanding, then traffic wearing away the weakened material. They range from small, shallow dips to large, deep craters that can cause significant damage to vehicles and pose serious safety risks for drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians.

Potholes form through a cycle: water penetrates the road surface through cracks, freeze-thaw cycles expand the water and weaken the pavement, and repeated traffic loads break the surface apart. They are most common in regions with cold winters and heavy rainfall.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about pothole detection

How accurate is the pothole detection?
Our YOLOv8 model achieves over 95% accuracy in detecting potholes under normal daylight conditions. Night mode detection uses AI image enhancement to maintain accuracy in low-light environments.
Can I use this on my mobile phone?
Yes! The system works on any device with a camera — desktop, tablet, or smartphone. Simply open the website, grant camera permissions, and start detecting potholes instantly.
Is my data and location private?
Yes, your privacy is important. Location data is only used for mapping pothole hotspots. Uploaded images are processed and stored securely. You can use the system without providing location.
How do I report potholes to authorities?
After detection, click "Submit Report" to file a citizen report. The system automatically generates a detailed report with images, GPS coordinates, severity analysis, and forwards it to the appropriate municipal authority.
What file formats are supported?
We support JPG, PNG images and MP4, AVI, MOV video files. For best results, use clear images with good lighting. The system also supports direct camera capture for real-time detection.
